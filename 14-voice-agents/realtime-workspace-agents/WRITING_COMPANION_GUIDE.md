### 3. **Substantive Editor** (Structure & Big Picture)
**Voice**: Direct, thoughtful, strategic

**Purpose**: Work on structure, clarity, argument, and flow

**How it works**:
- Reads your draft and identifies core issues
- Evaluates: Structure, Clarity, Completeness, Relevance, Flow
- Suggests big structural changes (reorder sections, cut tangents, add missing pieces)
- Strengthens arguments and logic
- Works at paragraph/section level (not sentences yet)

**Start here when**:
- You have a draft that feels messy or unfocused
- Your structure doesn't flow logically
- You need help organizing your thoughts
- Your argument needs strengthening
- Sections feel disconnected

**What they fix**:
- Buried leads (your best point is hidden)
- Missing connections between sections
- Weak or unclear arguments
- Tangents that don't serve the piece
- Kitchen sink syndrome (trying to cover too much)

---

### 4. **Line Editor** (Sentence-Level Flow)
**Voice**: Precise, rhythmic, attuned to language

**Purpose**: Make your prose sing at the sentence level

**How it works**:
- Improves sentence variety and rhythm
- Strengthens word choice and voice
- Cuts unnecessary words (tightens prose)
- Varies sentence length for better flow
- Ensures consistent tone

**Start here when**:
- Structure is solid but sentences feel clunky
- Writing feels repetitive or monotonous
- You want to strengthen your voice
- Prose feels too wordy or flat
- You need to polish sentence-level flow

**What they fix**:
- Too many similar sentence structures
- Passive voice ("was done by" â†’ active)
- Weak verbs ("is/was/were" â†’ action verbs)
- Filler words ("very", "really", "just", "quite")
- Monotonous rhythm (all sentences same length)

**Before/After Example**:
```
Before: "She was very angry at him because of what he said to her."
After:  "His words ignited her fury."
```

### 5. **Copyeditor** (Grammar & Consistency)
**Voice**: Precise, methodical, detail-oriented

**Purpose**: Fix grammar, punctuation, spelling, and consistency

**How it works**:
- Systematically checks grammar and syntax
- Corrects spelling and punctuation
- Ensures internal consistency (style, formatting)
- Explains corrections so you learn
- Verifies facts/citations if applicable

**Start here when**:
- Sentence-level flow is good but you need technical cleanup
- You want to catch grammar errors
- You need consistency checks
- You're not sure about grammar rules
- You're preparing for final polish

**What they fix**:
- Subject-verb agreement
- Its vs it's, their vs there vs they're
- Comma placement
- Consistent capitalization
- Oxford comma consistency
- Number formatting (spell out or digits?)

### 6. **Proofreader** (Final Polish)
**Voice**: Meticulous, eagle-eyed, final gatekeeper

**Purpose**: Last check before publication

**How it works**:
- Final read-through with fresh eyes
- Catches typos and awkward phrasings
- Verifies formatting consistency
- Checks that all edits were applied correctly
- Gives green light (or flags final issues)

**Start here when**:
- You've been through all other editing stages
- You think the piece is ready to publish
- You want one final check for typos
- You need fresh eyes on the final draft

**What they catch**:
- Typos that slipped through ("teh" â†’ "the")
- Double words ("the the")
- Missing punctuation
- Formatting inconsistencies
- Awkward phrasings that need one more pass
- Any final polish before going live

## ğŸ’¡ Tips for Best Results

### For Ideation:
- Follow what feels FUN, not what you "should" write
- Trust genuine curiosity over forced topics
- Short sessions (2-5 min) work best
- Your energy signals the right direction

### For Free-Writing:
- Don't stop! Keep hand moving
- Grammar doesn't matter
- Spelling doesn't matter
- Making sense doesn't matter
- The good stuff comes when you write past the surface

### For Substantive Editing:
- Don't wordsmith yet! Structure first
- Be willing to cut sections that don't serve the piece
- Front-load your best material (don't bury the lead)
- Each section should connect logically

### For Line Editing:
- Read sentences aloud (mentally or actually)
- Vary sentence length: Short. Medium. Long, flowing sentences.
- Strong verbs > weak verbs + adverbs
- Cut mercilessly (tight prose hits harder)

### For Copyediting:
- Let the agent teach you grammar rules
- Pick a style and stay consistent
- Don't over-correct style (respect the voice)

### For Proofreading:
- Fresh eyes catch things
- Trust your instinct - if something feels off, it probably is
- Don't rush this - final polish matters
